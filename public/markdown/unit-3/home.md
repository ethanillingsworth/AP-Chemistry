# Unit 3 | Substances & Mixtures

**Exam Weight:** 18–22% of the AP Exam

Unit 3 transitions from the intramolecular forces (bonds within molecules) to intermolecular forces (attractions between molecules). It explains how these forces dictate the physical properties of matter—solids, liquids, and gases—and explores the behavior of solutions, gas laws, and the interaction of light with matter.

## [3.1 Intermolecular Forces](/unit-3/topic-3.1)

- **London Dispersion Forces (LDFs):** Present in all molecules and atoms; strength increases with greater polarizability and a larger electron cloud.
- **Dipole-Dipole Interactions:** Occur between polar molecules; strength depends on the magnitude of the molecules' dipoles.
- **Hydrogen Bonding:** An unusually strong type of dipole-dipole force occurring when hydrogen is covalently bonded to a highly electronegative atom (N, O, or F).
- **Ion-Dipole Forces:** Forces of attraction between an ion and a polar molecule, commonly observed in solutions.

## [3.2 Properties of Solids](/unit-3/topic-3.2)

- **Ionic Solids:** High melting points, brittle, conduct electricity only when molten or dissolved in water.
- **Covalent Network Solids:** Consist of atoms covalently bonded in a continuous 3D network (e.g., diamond, quartz); characterized by extremely high melting points and hardness.
- **Molecular Solids:** Held together by IMFs; generally have relatively low melting points and are poor conductors.
- **Metallic Solids:** Form a "sea of electrons" making them malleable, ductile, and excellent conductors of heat and electricity.

## [3.3 Solids, Liquids, and Gases](/unit-3/topic-3.3)

- **Phases of Matter:** The state of matter depends on the balance between the kinetic energy of particles and the strength of the intermolecular forces between them.
- **Phase Changes:** Transitions between states (e.g., melting, boiling) require breaking or forming IMFs, not covalent bonds.

## [3.4 Ideal Gas Law](/unit-3/topic-3.4)

- **The Equation:** $PV = nRT$ relates the macroscopic properties of pressure, volume, moles, and temperature of an ideal gas.
- **Macroscopic Relationships:** Explains Boyle's, Charles's, and Avogadro's laws conceptually and mathematically.

## [3.5 Kinetic Molecular Theory](/unit-3/topic-3.5)

- **Particle Motion:** Gases consist of particles in constant, random motion.
- **Temperature and Kinetic Energy:** The Kelvin temperature of a gas is directly proportional to the average kinetic energy of its particles.
- **Maxwell-Boltzmann Distributions:** Graphical representations showing the distribution of particle speeds at different temperatures or for gases of different molar masses.

## [3.6 Deviation from Ideal Gas Law](/unit-3/topic-3.6)

- **Non-Ideal Behavior:** Gases deviate from ideal behavior most significantly at high pressures and low temperatures.
- **Causes of Deviation:** Real gas particles have a finite volume and experience intermolecular attractions, causing deviations from the ideal gas model.

## [3.7 Solutions and Mixtures](/unit-3/topic-3.7)

- **Molarity:** The standard unit of concentration, defined as moles of solute per liter of solution ($M = n/V$).
- **Solution Preparation:** Calculating the amounts needed to prepare solutions of specific molarities or to dilute concentrated stock solutions.

## [3.8 Representations of Solutions](/unit-3/topic-3.8)

- **Particulate Models:** Drawing and interpreting diagrams showing the interactions between solute and solvent particles, such as hydration shells around ions.

## [3.9 Separation of Solutions and Mixtures Chromatography](/unit-3/topic-3.9)

- **Separation Methods:** Techniques like distillation separate components based on differences in vapor pressure and boiling point (which rely on IMFs).
- **Chromatography:** Separates chemical species based on their relative affinities for a stationary phase versus a mobile phase.

## [3.10 Solubility](/unit-3/topic-3.10)

- **"Like Dissolves Like":** Substances with similar types of intermolecular forces tend to be soluble in one another.
- **Dissolution Process:** Involves overcoming solute-solute and solvent-solvent IMFs, and forming new solute-solvent interactions.

## [3.11 Spectroscopy and the Electromagnetic Spectrum](/unit-3/topic-3.11)

- **Light and Matter:** Different regions of the electromagnetic spectrum interact with matter in specific ways.
- **Types of Transitions:** Microwave radiation affects molecular rotation, infrared affects molecular vibration, and UV/visible light affects electron transitions.

## [3.12 Photoelectric Effect](/unit-3/topic-3.12)

- **Photon Energy:** Light acts as packets of energy (photons); the energy is proportional to its frequency ($E = h\nu$).
- **Electron Emission:** If a photon has sufficient energy, it can eject an electron from a material.

## [3.13 Beer-Lambert Law](/unit-3/topic-3.13)

- **Absorbance and Concentration:** The absorbance of a solution is directly proportional to its concentration ($A = \epsilon bc$).
- **Spectrophotometry:** Using the Beer-Lambert law to determine the concentration of an unknown colored solution based on the amount of light it absorbs.
