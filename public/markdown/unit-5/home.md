# Unit 5 | Kinetics

**Exam Weight:** 7–9% of the AP Exam

Unit 5 focuses on chemical kinetics, which is the study of the rates at which chemical reactions occur and the factors that affect these rates. It connects macroscopic observations of concentration changes over time to particulate-level mechanisms and the energy required for reactions to proceed.

## [5.1 Reaction Rates](/unit-5/topic-5.1)

- **Rate Definition:** The rate of a reaction is measured as the change in concentration of a reactant or product per unit of time.
- **Factors Affecting Rate:** Reaction rates are influenced by reactant concentration, temperature, surface area, and the presence of a catalyst.
- **Relative Rates:** The rate of disappearance of reactants and the rate of appearance of products are related by the stoichiometry of the balanced chemical equation.

## [5.2 Introduction to Rate Law](/unit-5/topic-5.2)

- **Rate Law Expression:** An equation relating the initial rate of a reaction to the concentration of reactants and the specific rate constant ($k$).
- **Reaction Order:** The power to which the concentration of a reactant is raised in the rate law (e.g., zero, first, or second order), which must be determined experimentally.
- **Rate Constant ($k$):** A proportionality constant whose units depend on the overall order of the reaction and whose value is highly temperature-dependent.

## [5.3 Concentration Changes Over Time](/unit-5/topic-5.3)

- **Integrated Rate Laws:** Mathematical expressions that relate the concentration of a reactant to time.
- **Graphical Analysis:** Linear graphs are used to determine reaction order: $[A]$ vs. time for zero-order, $\ln[A]$ vs. time for first-order, and $1/[A]$ vs. time for second-order.
- **Half-Life:** The time required for the concentration of a reactant to decrease to half its initial value; for a first-order reaction, the half-life is constant and independent of concentration.

## [5.4 Elementary Reactions](/unit-5/topic-5.4)

- **Elementary Steps:** Reactions that occur in a single event or step.
- **Molecularity:** The number of reacting particles involved in a single fundamental step (unimolecular, bimolecular, or rarely termolecular).
- **Rate Law of Elementary Step:** Unlike overall reactions, the rate law for an elementary step can be written directly from the stoichiometry of its balanced equation.

## [5.5 Collision Model](/unit-5/topic-5.5)

- **Effective Collisions:** For a reaction to occur, particles must collide with sufficient energy (activation energy) and the proper spatial orientation.
- **Temperature Effect:** Increasing temperature increases the average kinetic energy of the particles, leading to a higher collision frequency and a significantly larger fraction of collisions that exceed the activation energy.
- **Maxwell-Boltzmann Distribution:** Used to illustrate how an increase in temperature shifts the distribution of particle energies, resulting in more particles having energy greater than the activation energy.

## [5.6 Reaction Energy Profile](/unit-5/topic-5.6)

- **Energy Diagrams:** Graphs showing potential energy versus reaction progress.
- **Activation Energy ($E_a$):** The energy barrier that must be overcome for reactants to reach the transition state (activated complex) and become products.
- **Enthalpy Change ($\Delta H$):** The difference in potential energy between the products and the reactants.

## [5.7 Introduction to Reaction Mechanisms](/unit-5/topic-5.7)

- **Mechanisms:** A sequence of elementary steps by which an overall chemical reaction occurs.
- **Intermediates:** Chemical species that are produced in one step of a mechanism and completely consumed in a subsequent step, never appearing in the overall balanced equation.

## [5.8 Reaction Mechanism and Rate Law](/unit-5/topic-5.8)

- **Rate-Determining Step:** The slowest step in a reaction mechanism, which governs the overall rate law for the reaction.
- **Validating Mechanisms:** A proposed mechanism must sum to the overall balanced equation, and the rate law derived from its rate-determining step must align with the experimentally determined rate law.

## [5.9 Steady-State Approximation](/unit-5/topic-5.9)

- **Fast Initial Step:** When the first step in a mechanism is fast and reversible, an equilibrium approximation is often used to substitute intermediates in the rate-determining step to derive the final rate law.

## [5.10 Multistep Reaction Energy Profile](/unit-5/topic-5.10)

- **Complex Profiles:** Energy profiles for multistep reactions feature multiple peaks (each representing a transition state) and valleys (representing reactive intermediates).
- **Identifying the Slow Step:** The step with the highest activation energy peak relative to the energy of its reactants represents the rate-determining step of the mechanism.

## [5.11 Catalysis](/unit-5/topic-5.11)

- **Function of a Catalyst:** Increases the reaction rate by providing an alternative reaction pathway with a lower activation energy.
- **Catalyst Recovery:** Catalysts are consumed in an early step of a mechanism and regenerated in a later step, appearing in the overall reaction but not permanently consumed.
- **Energy Profile Impact:** A catalyst lowers the peak(s) on a reaction energy profile without changing the overall $\Delta H$ or thermodynamics of the reaction.
