# Unit 9 | Thermodynamics

**Exam Weight:** 7–9% of the AP Exam

Unit 9 ties together the concepts of thermodynamics, equilibrium, and kinetics. It introduces entropy and Gibbs free energy to predict the thermodynamic favorability of a process. The unit concludes with electrochemistry, exploring how thermodynamically favorable electron transfers can generate electricity and how electricity can drive unfavorable reactions.

## [9.1 Introduction to Entropy](/unit-9/topic-9.1)

- **Entropy ($S$):** A measure of the dispersal of matter and energy in a system.
- **Predicting Entropy Changes ($\Delta S$):** Entropy generally increases when matter becomes more dispersed (e.g., solid to liquid to gas), when the number of moles of gaseous products exceeds reactants, or when temperature increases.

## [9.2 Absolute Entropy and Entropy Change](/unit-9/topic-9.2)

- **Standard Entropies:** Every substance has an absolute standard entropy value ($S^\circ$) at a given temperature.
- **Calculating $\Delta S^\circ_{rxn}$:** The standard entropy change for a reaction is calculated by subtracting the sum of the standard entropies of the reactants from the sum of the standard entropies of the products: $\Delta S^\circ_{rxn} = \Sigma S^\circ(\text{Products}) - \Sigma S^\circ(\text{Reactants})$.

## [9.3 Gibbs Free Energy and Thermodynamic Favorability](/unit-9/topic-9.3)

- **Thermodynamic Favorability:** A process is considered thermodynamically favorable (spontaneous) if it can occur without ongoing outside intervention, indicated by a negative change in Gibbs free energy ($\Delta G < 0$).
- **The Gibbs Equation:** $\Delta G^\circ = \Delta H^\circ - T\Delta S^\circ$ relates the enthalpy, entropy, and absolute temperature (in Kelvin) of a system to its thermodynamic favorability.

## [9.4 Thermodynamic and Kinetic Control](/unit-9/topic-9.4)

- **Kinetic Control:** A thermodynamically favorable reaction ($\Delta G < 0$) may not occur at a measurable rate if its activation energy ($E_a$) is extremely high. Such reactions are said to be under kinetic control.

## [9.5 Free Energy and Equilibrium](/unit-9/topic-9.5)

- **Relating $\Delta G^\circ$ and $K$:** The standard free energy change is directly related to the equilibrium constant by the equation $\Delta G^\circ = -RT \ln K$.
- **Interpretation:** If $\Delta G^\circ < 0$, then $K > 1$ (product-favored). If $\Delta G^\circ > 0$, then $K < 1$ (reactant-favored). At equilibrium, $\Delta G = 0$.

## [9.6 Coupled Reactions](/unit-9/topic-9.6)

- **Driving Unfavorable Reactions:** A thermodynamically unfavorable reaction ($\Delta G^\circ > 0$) can be driven forward by coupling it with a highly favorable reaction ($\Delta G^\circ < 0$), provided they share a common intermediate and the overall sum of their $\Delta G^\circ$ values is negative.

## [9.7 Galvanic (Voltaic) and Electrolytic Cells](/unit-9/topic-9.7)

- **Electrochemical Cells:** Devices that involve a redox reaction, split into two half-cells (anode and cathode).
- **Anode and Cathode:** Oxidation always occurs at the anode, and reduction always occurs at the cathode (AN OX, RED CAT).
- **Galvanic vs. Electrolytic:** Galvanic cells house thermodynamically favorable reactions to produce electrical energy. Electrolytic cells require a power source to drive thermodynamically unfavorable reactions.

## [9.8 Cell Potential and Free Energy](/unit-9/topic-9.8)

- **Standard Cell Potential ($E^\circ_{cell}$):** Calculated using standard reduction potentials: $E^\circ_{cell} = E^\circ_{cathode} - E^\circ_{anode}$.
- **Relating $E^\circ$ to $\Delta G^\circ$:** The equation $\Delta G^\circ = -nFE^\circ_{cell}$ demonstrates that a thermodynamically favorable reaction ($\Delta G^\circ < 0$) must have a positive standard cell potential ($E^\circ_{cell} > 0$).

## [9.9 Cell Potential Under Nonstandard Conditions](/unit-9/topic-9.9)

- **The Nernst Equation Concept:** When conditions are not standard (concentrations $\neq 1 \text{ M}$, pressures $\neq 1 \text{ atm}$), the cell potential ($E_{cell}$) changes.
- **Qualitative Predictions:** Le Châtelier's principle and the reaction quotient ($Q$) can be used to predict how shifts in equilibrium will affect the cell potential. If the shift is toward products, $E_{cell}$ increases; if toward reactants, $E_{cell}$ decreases.

## [9.10 Electrolysis and Faraday's Law](/unit-9/topic-9.10)

- **Faraday's Laws:** The amount of chemical change (mass of substance plated or gas evolved) in an electrolytic cell is proportional to the amount of charge that passes through the cell.
- **Calculations:** Current ($I = q/t$) and Faraday's constant ($F \approx 96,485 \text{ C/mol } e^-$) are used in dimensional analysis to calculate the mass of a metal plated out during electroplating.
